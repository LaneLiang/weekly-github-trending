# Paper A Literature Quality Policy

This policy records the user's preferred venue categories and converts them into a strict screening rule for Paper A.

## Screening Tiers

| Tier | Meaning | Allowed claim strength |
|---|---|---|
| Preferred-core | User-preferred journals: TPE, TIE, TCAS-I, TCAS-II, JSSC, Nature, Science | May support synthesis, trend, comparative, and readiness claims after source-level verification |
| Preferred-conference | User-preferred conferences: APEC, ECCE, ISSCC | May support emerging-method and proof-of-concept claims, but not mature-readiness claims unless hardware evidence is strong |
| Supplementary-high-quality | Strong relevant archival journals not in the user's list, such as JESTPE or IEEE Open Journal of Power Electronics | May be used when directly relevant, but must be labelled as supplementary and not dominate the evidence base |
| Adjacent-context | Adjacent IEEE journals or non-power-electronics journals | May support comparison, transfer, or background only |
| Exclude-by-default | Low-fit venues, unrelated application domains, or conference papers outside preferred conference categories | Remove from Paper A unless the paper is uniquely necessary and explicitly justified |

## Non-Negotiable Rules

- A paper's algorithm label must be verified from the original source before it can appear in a table or figure.
- Conference papers outside APEC/ECCE/ISSCC are excluded by default from Paper A's main evidence base.
- IEEE Access, Sensors, Electronics, Ocean Engineering, Engineering Applications of Artificial Intelligence, and similar venues are not preferred-core evidence for Paper A. They can remain only as adjacent context or be removed.
- JESTPE is not in the user's explicit preferred list, but it is a high-quality power-electronics journal. It is treated as supplementary-high-quality unless the user later promotes it to preferred-core.
- Paper A should prefer fewer, verified, high-quality papers over a large but noisy citation list.
